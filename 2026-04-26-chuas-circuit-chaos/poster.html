The Circuit That Proved
Chaos Hides in Simplest Electronics

Leon Chua built a five-component circuit in 1983 expecting ordinary nonlinear behavior. What appeared on the oscilloscope became the most famous chaotic attractor in electronics.

dx/dt = α(y − x − g(x)) g(x) = m₁x + ½(m₀−m₁)(|x+1|−|x−1|)
dy/dt = x − y + z Classic params: α≈15.6, β≈28, m₀≈−1.143, m₁≈−0.714
