# 6 Free Audio Tools for Professional Editing

If you're a podcaster, musician, or content creator, you've probably spent hundreds on audio software just to trim a clip or remove background noise. There's a better way.

ElysiaTools just released 6 completely free browser-based audio tools that handle everything from precision cutting to noise reduction — no signup, no plugins, no watermarks.

---

## 1. Audio Cutter

**Cut clean clips in seconds.**

Upload any MP3, WAV, FLAC, AAC, OGG, or M4A. Enter your start and end time (in seconds or mm:ss), pick your output format, and download. It works directly in the browser with zero server-side processing delays.

Perfect for extracting a 30-second highlight from a 2-hour podcast interview.

**Use it:** https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/audio-cutter

---

## 2. Audio Format Converter

**Convert between any format without quality loss.**

This tool handles MP3, WAV, FLAC, AAC, OGG, and M4A with configurable bitrate (64k–320k), sample rate (22.05k–96k), stereo/mono channels, and volume adjustment.

The interface shows you exactly what settings you're applying before you commit. No surprise downgrades.

**Use it:** https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/audio-converter

---

## 3. Audio Mixer

**Overlay two tracks with frame-accurate timing.**

Select two audio files, set when the second track starts (0 = simultaneous, 1:30 = 90 seconds in), and mix. Supports all major formats.

This means you can layer background music under a voiceover without manual sync work.

**Use it:** https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/audio-mixer

---

## 4. Audio Splicer

**Merge multiple files into one.**

Unlike the mixer, the splicer concatenates 2–10 audio files in sequence. You can also apply crossfade (1, 2, 3, or 5 seconds) to smooth transitions between clips.

Useful for stitching together chapter recordings or assembling sound effects into a single file.

**Use it:** https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/audio-splicer

---

## 5. Audio Noise Reducer

**Remove hiss, hum, and background noise automatically.**

Choose from 6 algorithms: FFT Denoiser, High-Pass Filter (for low-frequency rumble), Low-Pass Filter (for high-frequency hiss), Combined (all three), Mild, or Aggressive.

The FFT Denoiser uses machine learning models to distinguish speech from noise — so you can clean a phone recording without turning your voice into a robot.

Adjustable reduction level (0.1–1.0) lets you preserve as much audio detail as you need.

**Use it:** https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/audio-noise-reducer

---

## 6. Audio Speed Changer

**Change tempo without changing pitch.**

Adjust playback speed from 0.25x (quarter speed) to 4.0x (quadruple speed) while keeping the original pitch intact. Uses time-stretching algorithms rather than simple resampling, so vocals stay natural even at 0.5x.

Great for slowing down reference tracks for transcription or speeding up audio for time-constrained tutorials.

**Use it:** https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/audio-speed-changer

---

## What's Missing

These 6 tools cover the core editing workflow: cut → convert → mix → merge → clean → adjust speed. But there's no batch processing mode yet — if you need to normalize 200 audio files at once, you're still doing it one at a time.

That's the next gap ElysiaTools could fill.
