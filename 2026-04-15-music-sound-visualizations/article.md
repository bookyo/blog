# 6 Music & Sound Theory Visualizations That Will Transform How You Hear the World

Every note you hear is the result of physics you can't see. Frequency ratios, wave interference, doppler shifts — they're invisible to your ears, but not to these visualizations. Here's 6 free browser-based tools that make sound theory actually click.

---

## 1. Musical Intervals & Frequency Ratios

When you hear two notes together, why does a perfect fifth (3:2 ratio) sound harmonious while others feel "off"? This tool lets you hear and see exactly why.

Play unison (1:1) and watch the waves perfectly overlap. Then shift to a minor sixth (8:5) and watch the chaos. You'll see the mathematical reason musicians talk about " consonance" — it's about harmonic alignment, not arbitrary taste.

**Try it:** [Musical Intervals & Frequency Ratios →](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/musical-intervals)

---

## 2. Lissajous Figures

Oscilloscopes display these elegant curves when they combine two perpendicular signals. A 1:1 frequency ratio with 90° phase shift produces a perfect circle. Change it to 1:2 and you get a figure-8. These patterns were once used to tune orchestral instruments — if the Lissajous figure was stable, the note was in tune.

**Try it:** [Lissajous Figures →](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/lissajous-figures)

---

## 3. Beat Frequency

Play two tones close in frequency — say 440Hz and 442Hz — and you'll hear a pulsing "wub-wub-wub" at 2Hz. That's the beat frequency. This is how musicians tune by ear: when the beats disappear, the notes match. It's also the same phenomenon behind the swelling sound you hear from a distant drone.

**Try it:** [Beat Frequency →](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/beat-frequency)

---

## 4. Doppler Effect

The same effect that makes an ambulance siren pitch-shift as it passes you at 70mph is captured here in real-time. Adjust the source velocity, hear the frequency climb and crash as the source crosses your position. You can also switch to the observer-moving scenario — a receiver walking toward a stationary source hears a higher pitch too.

**Try it:** [Doppler Effect →](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/doppler-effect)

---

## 5. Sound Attenuation

Sound doesn't travel forever. It fades due to geometric spreading, air absorption, and scattering. This visualization shows you exactly how SPL (sound pressure level) drops with distance — critical for concert venue design, noise ordinances, and even video game audio engineering.

**Try it:** [Sound Attenuation →](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/sound-attenuation)

---

## 6. Wave Superposition

When two waves occupy the same medium, they don't collide — they pass through each other, adding their amplitudes at every point. This creates interference: constructive (louder) and destructive (quieter) zones. It's the principle behind noise-cancelling headphones, concert venue acoustics, and the woodwind instruments' unique timbre.

**Try it:** [Wave Superposition →](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/wave-superposition)
