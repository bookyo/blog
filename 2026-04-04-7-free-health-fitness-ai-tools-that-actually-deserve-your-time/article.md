## AI Telomere Repair Exercise Generator

Telomeres are the protective caps on your chromosomes. Their length is a biological marker of cellular aging. Exercise science — peer-reviewed exercise science, not supplement marketing — shows that consistent aerobic and resistance training can increase telomere length and boost telomerase activity.

This tool takes your age, current fitness level, available time, and equipment access, then builds a weekly program structured around the three exercise modalities linked to telomere health: aerobic endurance, HIIT, and resistance training. Research suggests telomerase activity — the enzyme that repairs and lengthens telomeres — can roughly double after six months of consistent exercise.

Each day specifies exercise type, duration, intensity, warm-up, and cool-down. It also covers electrolyte supplementation and sleep optimization because both affect cellular repair.

This won't turn back the clock. But it's a structured program grounded in longevity research — not whatever a fitness influencer posted this week.

**Use it**: [AI Telomere Repair Exercise Generator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/ai-telomere-exercise-generator)

## BMI Calculator

The Body Mass Index is an imperfect tool. It doesn't distinguish muscle from fat, and it performs poorly across ethnic groups. But it's still the most widely used screening metric for weight categories, and knowing where you fall takes under 20 seconds.

Enter weight, height, and unit system. Get your BMI number, category (Underweight / Normal / Overweight / Obese), an ideal weight range for your height, a health risk assessment, and three to four specific recommendations based on your category.

It doesn't try to be more than it is. That's exactly what makes it useful.

**Use it**: [BMI Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/bmi-calculator)

## BMR Calculator

Basal Metabolic Rate is the calories your body burns at complete rest. Knowing it is the foundation of any serious nutrition plan — weight loss, muscle gain, or maintenance — because it tells you the baseline your body needs just to function.

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation, which most clinical nutritionists consider the most accurate of the standard BMR formulas. Input: gender, height, weight, and age. Output: your BMR in calories per day, explained in plain language.

Pair it with a TDEE calculator and you have the skeleton of a personalized calorie target. No subscription. No phone number.

**Use it**: [BMR Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/bmr-calculator)

## TDEE Calculator

BMR is what your body burns at rest. TDEE is what it burns when you add everything else — walking, working out, digesting food, fidgeting.

This TDEE Calculator takes your BMR and multiplies it by an activity multiplier to produce a daily calorie number you can actually use. The activity categories are specific enough to matter: sedentary, lightly active, moderately active, highly active, extremely active. Those definitions make or break the accuracy.

Once you know your TDEE, weight management stops being a guessing game. It becomes arithmetic.

**Use it**: [TDEE Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/tdee-calculator)

## Calorie Burned Calculator

Running "30 minutes" burns somewhere between 200 and 600 calories depending on your weight, pace, terrain, and a dozen other variables. A 150-pound person running 5 miles at 10-minute pace burns roughly 400 calories. The same run at 220 pounds burns closer to 580. Most free calculators use a population average and miss by 30-40%.

This one asks for your weight, activity type (running, cycling, swimming, walking, and others), duration, and intensity, then applies weight-specific formulas to produce a number relevant to you.

Use it to compare activities honestly. If you're trying to create a caloric deficit through exercise rather than just cutting food, this level of specificity matters.

**Use it**: [Calorie Burned Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/calorie-burned-calculator)

## Sleep Cycle Calculator

Poor sleep sabotages willpower, recovery, muscle protein synthesis, and fat oxidation. Most people know they should sleep more. Fewer know that waking mid-cycle produces more grogginess than sleeping less total time.

This calculator works backward from your wake-up time to tell you the optimal bedtime, based on the average 90-minute sleep cycle. It also shows you what happens if you go to bed at alternative times, so you can make an informed decision about that second episode.

Set your alarm for 6:30 AM. The tool tells you 8:00 PM or 9:30 PM are your optimal bedtimes. No app, no wearable required.

**Use it**: [Sleep Cycle Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/sleep-cycle-calculator)
