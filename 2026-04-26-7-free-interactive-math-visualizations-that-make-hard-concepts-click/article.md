## 1. [Benford's Law](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/benfords-law) — The Fraud Detection Formula

Did you know that in naturally occurring numbers, **1 appears as the first digit about 30% of the time**, while 9 appears only 4.6%? That's Benford's Law.

**The formula:** P(d) = log₁₀(1 + 1/d), for d = 1, 2, ..., 9

This visualization lets you:
- Test the law against real datasets (world population, GDP, Fibonacci numbers)
- Upload your own data and run a chi-squared goodness-of-fit test
- See why uniform random numbers *don't* follow this law

**Why it matters:** Auditors use this to detect fraudulent financial statements. Scientists use it to spot data fabrication. A single test tells you whether numbers are naturally generated or artificially constructed.

Try it: [https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/benfords-law](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/benfords-law)

## 2. [Fourier Series](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/fourier-series) — Any Wave Built from Circles

The counterintuitive truth: **any periodic function is just a sum of sine waves**. But watching epicycles trace out a square wave? That's when it clicks.

This visualization shows:
- Epicycles (rotating circles) that draw your waveform
- Adjustable number of terms — watch how more circles = better approximation
- Your own custom-drawn curves analyzed via Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT)

**The magic moment:** With just 10 terms, you get the rough shape. At 50 terms, it looks nearly perfect — except at discontinuities, where Gibbs phenomenon shows up as overshoot.

**Why it matters:** Fourier analysis underlies audio compression (MP3), image compression (JPEG), 5G communication, and quantum mechanics.

Try it: [https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/fourier-series](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/fourier-series)

## 3. [Conway's Game of Life](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/game-of-life) — Turing's Complete Universe

Four rules. Infinite complexity.

- Neighbors < 2 → death (underpopulation)
- Neighbors 2-3 → survival
- Neighbors > 3 → death (overpopulation)
- Dead cell + exactly 3 neighbors → reproduction

This visualization includes:
- Preset patterns: Glider, Pulsar, Gosper Glider Gun
- Adjustable grid size, speed, and initial density
- Statistics tracking (max population, growth rate)

**Why it matters:** Game of Life is Turing complete — you can build a computer inside it. Some patterns generate prime numbers. It's the clearest example of how simple rules create emergent complexity.

Try it: [https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/game-of-life](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/game-of-life)

## 4. [Entropy](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/entropy) — Why Time Flows One Way

Entropy is the only physical law where time has a direction. This visualization simulates:
- Particle diffusion in an isolated system
- Maxwell's Demon (and its thermodynamic cost via Landauer's Principle)
- Energy injection in open systems

**Key insight:** The total entropy (system + information) never decreases. A refrigerator cools your room by increasing the total entropy of the universe.

**The formula:** S = k·ln(Ω) — Boltzmann's entropy, where Ω is the number of microstates.

**Why it matters:** Entropy explains why time flows forward, why life requires constant energy input, and why black holes have entropy (Bekenstein-Hawking).

Try it: [https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/entropy](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/entropy)

**Why it matters:** The same equations that govern a double pendulum describe weather patterns, stock markets, and biological populations. Chaos isn't randomness — it's hidden order with extreme sensitivity.

## 6. [Law of Cosines](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/law-of-cosines) — Beyond Pythagoras

For any triangle (not just right triangles):

**c² = a² + b² - 2ab·cos(C)**

Drag the vertices, watch the formula update in real-time. See step-by-step derivation. The moment you drag a vertex and the formula *instantly* recalculates — that's when it clicks.

**Why it matters:** GPS triangulation, architectural structural analysis, and navigation all rely on this. It's the generalization of Pythagoras that your geometry teacher didn't show you.

## 7. [Lorenz Attractor](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/lorenz-attractor) — The Butterfly Effect Visualized

The system:
- dx/dt = σ(y - x)
- dy/dt = x(ρ - z) - y
- dz/dt = xy - βz

With σ=10, ρ=28, β=8/3, you get the most iconic chaotic system in mathematics.

This visualization shows:
- 3D trajectory that never intersects itself
- The strange attractor shape
- Sensitivity to initial conditions

**Why it matters:** Edward Lorenz discovered this in 1963 while modeling weather. The "butterfly effect" isn't just a metaphor — it's a precise mathematical property of chaotic systems.

Try it: [https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/lorenz-attractor](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/lorenz-attractor)
