## 2. Amortization Schedule Generator

**What it does:** Breaks down every single loan payment into principal vs. interest — for mortgages, car loans, personal loans, or any amortized debt.

**Why it matters:** In a 30-year mortgage at 6%, roughly 70% of your early payments go to interest. Knowing exactly when you hit the "principal flip" — when more of your payment reduces the balance than pays interest — is a powerful moment to consider extra payments.

**Use it when:** You want to see how much total interest you'll pay, or plan bi-weekly payments to cut years off your mortgage.

**Inputs:** Loan amount, interest rate, loan term, start date, payment frequency  
**Output:** Month-by-month schedule, yearly summary, payoff date, total interest paid

## 3. Capital Gains Tax Calculator

**What it does:** Calculates exactly how much tax you owe on an investment sale — accounting for holding period (short-term vs. long-term), your income bracket, filing status, and country.

**Why it matters:** Selling a stock after 11 months vs. 13 months can mean the difference between paying your top income tax rate (37% in the US) vs. the long-term capital gains rate (0–20%). That's thousands of dollars on a $100,000 gain.

**Use it when:** You're planning to sell stocks, real estate, or crypto and want to optimize timing for tax efficiency.

**Inputs:** Purchase/sale price, holding period, annual income, filing status, country  
**Output:** Short-term vs. long-term classification, effective tax rate, after-tax profit

## 5. Debt Snowball Calculator

**What it does:** Models the "debt snowball" method — paying off your smallest balance first while making minimum payments on everything else, then rolling that payment into the next debt.

**Why it matters:** Mathematically, the "avalanche method" (highest interest first) saves the most money. But research shows the "snowball" — small wins early — produces higher follow-through rates. Momentum matters more than marginal interest savings for most people.

**Use it when:** You have multiple debts (credit cards, car loans, student loans) and want a clear payoff roadmap with actual dates.

**Inputs:** Total monthly payment available, up to 5 debts with balances/rates/minimums  
**Output:** Payoff order, payoff date for each debt, total interest paid, month-by-month schedule

## 6. Bond Yield Calculator

**What it does:** Calculates a bond's current yield and yield-to-maturity (YTM) — the real return you'd earn if you held the bond until it expires.

**Why it matters:** A bond trading at a discount (below face value) with a 6% coupon might actually yield 7.2% to maturity. Without calculating YTM, you'd miss that the market is pricing in a better deal than the coupon rate suggests.

**Use it when:** You're buying individual bonds or bond funds and want to compare them fairly against stocks, CDs, or high-yield savings accounts.

**Inputs:** Face value, coupon rate, current price, years to maturity, payment frequency  
**Output:** Current yield, YTM, annual coupon payment, premium/discount classification
