## 3. Backpropagation Deep Dive — How Networks Actually Learn

Backpropagation is the algorithm that makes neural networks *learn*. It propagates errors backward through the network and adjusts weights using gradient descent. Most courses gloss over it. This visualization doesn't.

**What this visualization shows you:**
- A step-by-step walkthrough of the backpropagation process
- The mathematical derivations rendered as interactive equations
- How gradients flow and shrink (or explode) through layers
- Practical engineering considerations: learning rates, gradient clipping, and common failure modes

This means you stop treating backprop as a black box and start understanding *why* training sometimes fails.

## 4. PCA and Eigenvectors — Making Sense of High-Dimensional Data

When your dataset has 1,000 features, you can't visualize it. PCA (Principal Component Analysis) finds the directions of maximum variance and projects your data onto a lower-dimensional space — without losing the signal.

**What this visualization shows you:**
- Covariance ellipses and how eigenvectors define them
- Interactive PCA: rotate axes, see how much variance each captures
- Why the first few principal components often contain 90%+ of the information
- Real-world intuition: what "dimensionality reduction" actually looks like

In other words, you learn why dimensionality reduction isn't just compression — it's *smart* compression that preserves what matters.

## 5. Markov Chains — Where Probability Meets Predictability

Markov chains are everywhere: search engines, speech recognition, genetics, and financial modeling. The core idea is elegant: the future depends only on the present, not the past.

**What this visualization shows you:**
- How probability flows between states over time
- Convergence to stationary distribution — watch the system settle into equilibrium
- Transition matrices visualized as weighted graphs
- Real examples: weather models, random walks, and text generation

This means you can model complex sequential systems with surprisingly simple mathematics.

## 6. Lotka-Volterra Predator-Prey Model — Population Dynamics

Ecological systems oscillate. Foxes eat rabbits → rabbit population drops → foxes starve → rabbit population recovers → foxes bounce back. This is the Lotka-Volterra equations at work.

**What this visualization shows you:**
- Phase plane diagrams showing predator and prey populations over time
- How changing birth rates and predation rates shifts the cycle
- Why these oscillations are *robust* — small perturbations don't derail them
- Connections to limit cycles and nonlinear dynamics in applied mathematics

These equations show up in economics, epidemiology, and neural network theory too. This visualization makes that connection visceral.

## 7. Ising Model — Phase Transitions and Critical Phenomena

The 2D Ising model is one of the most studied systems in statistical mechanics. At low temperature, spins align (ferromagnetism). Above a critical temperature, they randomize. That transition — a *phase transition* — shares deep mathematical similarities with neural network phase transitions.

**What this visualization shows you:**
- A live Monte Carlo simulation of spins on a 2D lattice
- The critical temperature (the Curie point) and what happens at the boundary
- How magnetization emerges from microscopic disorder
- Connections to computational neuroscience and mean-field theory

Understanding phase transitions gives you intuition for why large language models suddenly exhibit emergent capabilities at certain scale thresholds.

👉 [Try it free →](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/ising-model)

## 8. Gray Rhino Theory — Why We Ignore Obvious Risks

Named by author Michele Wucker, a "gray rhino" is a highly probable, high-impact threat that we systematically ignore. It's not a black swan (unforeseen) — it's the thing we see coming and fail to act on.

**What this visualization shows you:**
- Risk accumulation dynamics over time
- The gap between recognition and response — why knowing isn't enough
- How to structure incentives and decision frameworks to act on gray rhinos
- Connections to AI risk: compute scaling, alignment, and concentrated power

This visualization brings behavioral economics and decision theory to life in a way that sticks.
