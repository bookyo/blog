## 2. AI Telomere Repair Exercise Generator — Exercise That Targets Aging at the Cellular Level

This sounds like science fiction. Telomeres are protective caps on your chromosome ends — they shorten every time a cell divides. Shorter telomeres correlate with faster biological aging. The research question: can exercise preserve them?

Yes — but only certain types and intensities. Most people doing random workouts have no idea whether their routine actually moves the needle.

The [AI Telomere Repair Exercise Generator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/ai-telomere-exercise-generator) takes the science seriously. Based on your age, fitness level, available equipment, and weekly schedule, it builds a 7-day plan around three exercise modalities most linked to telomere health:

- **Aerobic endurance** (3× per week, 45 minutes): sustained moderate-intensity cardio. Peer-reviewed research shows this can double telomerase activity after 6 months.
- **HIIT** (2× per week): high-intensity intervals alternating with recovery, linked to elevated cellular repair.
- **Resistance training** (2× per week): muscle-preserving work that supports metabolic health and mitochondrial function.

The tool asks about your available equipment before prescribing anything — so the plan it generates is actually doable, not aspirational.

**Use case:** Over 35, training inconsistently, and you want a weekly plan with an actual biological rationale.
