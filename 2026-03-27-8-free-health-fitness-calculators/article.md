Every New Year's, the fitness app grind starts. People hand over email addresses, get hit with paywalls just to see their BMR, and end up back where they started.

The math for all of these calculations is the same science — you just need the right tool. Here's a curated set of **8 free health and fitness calculators** that do the job without the nonsense.

---

## 1. BMI Calculator

Body Mass Index is the first number most people look up when they start caring about health. It's simple: weight divided by height squared. But the *useful* version also gives you a category (Underweight / Normal / Overweight / Obese), an ideal weight range, and plain-English health recommendations.

**Use it when:** You want a quick snapshot of where your weight falls relative to population norms.

**[Try it → BMI Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/bmi-calculator)**

---

## 2. BMR Calculator

Your Basal Metabolic Rate is the calories your body burns at complete rest — just keeping you alive. It's the floor of your daily energy needs. This tool calculates it using the **Mifflin-St Jeor equation**, which studies show is the most accurate for most people, and also offers Harris-Benedict and Katch-McArdle as alternatives.

**Use it when:** You're building a nutrition plan from scratch and need a starting number.

**[Try it → BMR Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/bmr-calculator)**

---

## 3. TDEE Calculator

Total Daily Energy Expenditure is where things get real. BMR times your activity multiplier gives you what you actually burn in a day — desk job vs. CrossFit 6 days a week makes a *huge* difference. This tool uses multipliers from 1.2 (sedentary) to 1.9 (very active) to give you a number you can actually plan meals around.

**Use it when:** You want to know approximately how many calories you burn every single day.

**[Try it → TDEE Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/tdee-calculator)**

---

## 4. Daily Calorie Needs Calculator

This is the TDEE calculator's smarter sibling. It starts with your BMR, applies your activity level, and then — crucially — adjusts for your goal. Want to lose 0.5 kg/week? It subtracts 500 calories. Building muscle? It adds 250. It also breaks down your **daily macros** (protein, carbs, fat) automatically.

**Use it when:** You're ready to commit to a plan with a specific calorie target.

**[Try it → Daily Calorie Needs Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/daily-calorie-needs)**

## 5. Calorie Burned Calculator

Not all exercise is equal. This calculator handles a wide range of activities — from gardening and walking to swimming and rock climbing — and gives you calorie estimates based on your weight, duration, and intensity. One input, dozens of activities.

**Use it when:** You want to account for workouts you don't track elsewhere.

**[Try it → Calorie Burned Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/calorie-burned-calculator)**

## 6. Running Calorie Calculator

Running is one of the most calorie-intensive common activities, but the burn rate varies wildly based on pace and your weight. This calculator uses your distance or duration to estimate what a run actually costs you. Enter pace and it works backward — enter distance and it works the same way.

**Use it when:** You're training for a race and want to know how to refuel post-run.

**[Try it → Running Calorie Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/running-burn-calculator)**

## 7. Cycling Calorie Calculator

This tool lets you input distance or duration, choose your speed or let it calculate from distance + time, and pick your intensity level (light / moderate / vigorous) for a much better estimate than generic MET values.

**Use it when:** You're a cyclist and want to nail your nutrition on training days.

## The Workflow

These 8 tools form a complete chain:

1. **BMI** → Get oriented
2. **BMR** → Know your floor
3. **TDEE** → Know your daily burn
4. **Daily Calorie Needs** → Set your actual target with goal adjustment
5. **Running / Cycling / Calorie Burned** → Track workouts
6. **Macro Nutrient Calculator** → Plan your meals

No app. No account. No paywall.

## One Thing These Still Don't Solve

Calculators give you numbers. They don't tell you:

- Whether you're eating *enough protein* to actually retain muscle while cutting
- How to adjust when your weight loss stalls after 3 weeks
- The quality of your food — 2000 calories of donuts vs. 2000 calories of whole foods produce *very* different results

For those questions, you'll eventually need something more adaptive. But for getting your starting numbers right? These 8 are enough.
