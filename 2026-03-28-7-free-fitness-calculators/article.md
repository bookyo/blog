## 2. VO2 Max Calculator

VO2 Max is the gold standard for aerobic fitness — how much oxygen your body can consume per minute per kilogram of body weight. The problem: clinical testing is expensive.

This tool estimates your VO2 Max from three non-lab methods: Resting Heart Rate, Rockport 1-Mile Walk, or 1.5 Mile Run. All you need is a stopwatch.

**Use it when:** You want to track cardiovascular progress over months without a lab visit.

**Try it:** [VO2 Max Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/vo2-max-calculator)

## 3. Sleep Cycle Calculator

You can sleep 8 hours and still wake up feeling wrecked. The reason: you woke up in the middle of a deep sleep cycle.

This tool works backwards from a 90-minute sleep cycle — the average duration of one full cycle (light → deep → REM). Tell it when you want to wake up (or when you're going to sleep), and it calculates the optimal times to fall asleep or get up.

**Use it when:** You need to wake up feeling refreshed for an early meeting or a hard workout.

**Try it:** [Sleep Cycle Calculator](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/sleep-cycle-calculator)

**Use it when:** You keep forgetting to drink water and want a concrete number to hit.

## The Metric You Should Track Today

If you're only going to track one number, track your **resting heart rate**. Measure it first thing in the morning before you get out of bed. A declining resting HR over weeks means your aerobic base is improving. It's the cheapest fitness biomarker you can monitor — all you need is a clock.
