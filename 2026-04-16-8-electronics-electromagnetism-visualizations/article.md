## 2. RLC Circuit Oscillation

An RLC circuit combines resistance, inductance, and capacitance. This visualization shows damped harmonic motion, resonance peaks, and energy sloshing back and forth between the capacitor and inductor.

**Why it matters:** RLC circuits are the backbone of radio tuners, oscillators, and audio filters. The resonance frequency is where energy transfer peaks — critical knowledge for designing any frequency-selective circuit.

## 3. Lenz's Law Demonstration

Move a magnet through a coil and watch the galvanometer swing. Lenz's law says the induced current creates a magnetic field that opposes the change — which is why you feel resistance when pushing a magnet through copper pipe.

**Why it matters:** This single law explains transformers, electric generators, induction motors, electromagnetic braking, metal detectors, and wireless charging. It's one of the most practical laws in all of physics.

👉 [Explore Lenz's Law Demonstration →](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/lenz-law)

## 4. Electric Field Lines

Place point charges on a canvas and watch field lines arc from positive to negative. The density of lines shows field strength; the direction shows force on a positive test charge.

**Why it matters:** Electric field visualization is the foundation for understanding capacitors, semiconductors, and antenna radiation patterns. Engineers use these same principles to design everything from PCBs to MRI machines.

**Why it matters:** Understanding wave propagation is essential for antenna design, optical communications, and RF engineering. The relationship between E-field, B-field, and propagation direction is the basis for all wireless technology.

## 6. Optical Fiber Principle

Watch light ray-trace through a fiber optic strand. The visualization demonstrates total internal reflection, numerical aperture, and why light can travel miles of fiber with minimal loss.

**Why it matters:** Fiber optic cables carry 95% of the world's internet traffic. Total internal reflection — where light bounces off the core-cladding boundary at angles above the critical angle — is what makes low-loss long-distance communication possible.

👉 [Explore Optical Fiber Principle →](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/optical-fiber)

## 7. Lissajous Figures

When two sinusoidal signals drive the x and y axes simultaneously, you get Lissajous figures. The shape reveals the frequency ratio and phase difference between the signals — a relationship engineers have used since the 1850s.

**Why it matters:** Before digital oscilloscopes, Lissajous patterns were the primary way to measure phase difference and frequency ratios. Today they appear in oscilloscope displays, audio analysis, and harmonograph art. The figure-8 pattern at a 1:2 ratio is particularly striking.

👉 [Explore Lissajous Figures →](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/lissajous-figures)

## 8. Duffing Oscillator

The Duffing oscillator adds a nonlinear stiffness term to the harmonic oscillator equation. The result? A double-well potential that exhibits hysteresis, chaotic sensitivity to initial conditions, and period-doubling bifurcations.

**Why it matters:** The Duffing oscillator models structural vibrations in bridges and buildings, electronic circuits with nonlinear inductors, and even crowd-induced sway on pedestrian bridges. It's the simplest system that exhibits true deterministic chaos.
