## 1. Dice Roller — Tabletop Gaming Without the Clatter

Rolling physical dice is fine — until you need 12d6 for a boss fight and you only have one die.

The Dice Roller handles any combination: d4, d6, d8, d12, d20, even weird stuff like 3d10 or 17d6. You can set the number of dice and the number of faces independently.

**Use it for:** D&D sessions, board game replacements, probability experiments, classroom demos on randomness.

`https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/dice-roller`

## 2. Sudoku Generator — Fresh Puzzles at Your Difficulty Level

Sudoku is deceptively hard to create by hand. Getting a valid puzzle with a unique solution is even harder.

This generator produces brand-new Sudoku grids on demand. You control the difficulty by setting how many cells are pre-filled — fewer clues means harder puzzles. Each puzzle is validated to have exactly one solution.

**Use it for:** Morning routine, coffee break brain training, printing puzzles for long trips, competition prep.

`https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/sudoku-generator`

## 3. Sudoku Solver — When You're Stuck and Curious

Sometimes you're mid-puzzle and one cell just won't cooperate. Instead of erasing and starting over, feed the grid into the Solver.

It backtracking-validates the entire board and highlights the solution. If the puzzle is unsolvable or has multiple solutions, it tells you that too.

**Use it for:** Checking your own work, learning Sudoku techniques by comparing your approach to the solution, validating puzzles you found elsewhere.

`https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/sudoku-solver`

## 4. Bingo Card Generator — Custom Cards in Under a Minute

Bingo is one of those tools that quietly works for almost every context: classrooms, corporate events, party games, language learning.

This generator creates PDF bingo cards with your own words, numbers, or phrases. You set the grid size (3×3, 4×4, 5×5) and the tool randomizes the content across as many unique cards as you need. Print them out and you're ready.

**Use it for:** Classroom vocabulary review, team-building events, conference icebreakers, holiday party games.

`https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/bingo-card-generator`

## 5. Word Search Generator — Hide Words in a Grid, Your Way

A word search puzzle sounds simple, but laying out 15 words in a 15×15 grid without overlap — and hiding them in eight directions — is genuinely tedious to do by hand.

This generator takes your word list and builds the grid automatically. You control the grid size and the directions. It also gives you an answer key.

**Use it for:** Teaching vocabulary, foreign language practice, finding hidden words as a party challenge, birthday party printables.

`https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/word-search-generator`

Building a crossword by hand means placing letters that simultaneously form intersecting words — and making sure the whole thing is coherent.

This generator takes your words and clues and constructs the grid for you. The result is a valid crossword with interlocking entries and an answer key.

**Use it for:** Trivia events, newspaper-style puzzles, educational assessments, building custom puzzles around a specific theme.

`https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/crossword-generator`

## 7. Maze Generator — Labyrinths With Controllable Complexity

Mazes sit at the intersection of fun and educational. They're also surprisingly hard to draw well by hand — too simple and they're boring, too complex and they're frustrating.

You set the width, height, and difficulty. The generator produces a new randomized maze each time. Great for printing.

**Use it for:** Children's activity sheets, programming pathfinding examples, escape room clues, mindfulness coloring activities.

`https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/maze-generator`

## 8. Deck Shuffler — Simulate a Real Shuffle

Real card shuffles are surprisingly hard to replicate mentally. People consistently shuffle worse than they think — which matters in magic, poker analysis, and card game testing.

This shuffler randomizes a standard 52-card deck and shows you the result. You can shuffle multiple times and compare results.

**Use it for:** Card game prototyping, magic trick rehearsals, teaching probability, Monte Carlo simulations for poker odds.

`https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/deck-shuffler`
