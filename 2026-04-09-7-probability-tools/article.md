**What it does:**
- Specific hand lookup (e.g., what's the chance of a Four of a Kind?)

**Use it when:** You're about to play poker and want to calibrate your expectations. A Full House happens roughly 0.144% of the time — that's once every 693 hands.

## 2. Dice Probability Calculator

Rolling dice looks random. Mathematically, it is — but that doesn't mean every outcome is equally likely.

The Dice Probability Calculator handles any dice configuration (1–10 dice, 2–100 sides) and can answer five types of questions: specific value probability, "at least X," "at most X," exact sum, and full distribution.

**What it does:**
- Probability of rolling any specific value on a single die
- "At least X" / "at most X" calculations for multiple dice
- Full probability distribution across all possible sums
- Results as fraction, decimal, percentage, and "X:Y" odds

**Use it when:** You're designing a board game, adjudicating D&D rules, or settling an argument about whether "7 is the most common roll" (it is — because it's the sum with the most combinations).
