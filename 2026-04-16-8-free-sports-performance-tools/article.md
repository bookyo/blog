# 8 Free Sports & Performance Tools Every Athlete Needs in 2026

*Why guess when you can calculate?*

Most fitness apps give you a number and call it a day. Behind that number is a formula — and different activities need different formulas. Generic calorie trackers treat a 5K run the same as a 400m sprint. They don't account for stroke technique in swimming or progressive fatigue in a marathon.

These 8 free tools do the math properly.

---

## 1. Running Calorie Calculator

Running burns more calories per minute than almost any other cardio activity. But the exact number depends heavily on your pace. A 6 min/km jogger burns significantly fewer calories per km than someone running at 4 min/km — yet the distance is identical.

This tool uses **MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) values** adjusted for pace. You input weight, distance, and either duration or pace. The tool back-calculates whichever you omitted.

What makes it useful: it works in both metric and imperial units, and it outputs calories per km and per mile — useful for planning race nutrition.

**Use it for:** planning pre-race fueling, comparing training sessions, tracking weekly calorie expenditure.

## 2. Swimming Calorie Calculator

Swimming is unique among cardio activities because **stroke technique dramatically changes energy cost**. Butterfly and breaststroke require more total body engagement than freestyle — butterfly burns roughly 37% more calories per hour than leisure swimming at the same speed.

This tool factors in stroke type, intensity, duration, and optional distance. It outputs calories burned, calories per hour, and estimated average speed — which varies by stroke.

What makes it useful: most fitness trackers dramatically undercount swimming calories because they can't detect stroke type. This tool does it manually and accurately.

**Use it for:** planning pool training nutrition, comparing efficiency across strokes, tracking recovery swim energy expenditure.

[Try it →](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/swimming-burn-calculator)

## 3. Cycling Calorie Calculator

Cycling calorie calculations depend on three variables: **your weight, speed, and terrain difficulty**. A 70 kg person cycling at 20 km/h on flat ground burns roughly 560 calories per hour. The same person at 30 km/h burns nearly double — not because they're going 50% faster, but because aerodynamic drag increases with the cube of velocity.

This tool uses intensity-adjusted MET values (light/moderate/vigorous/very vigorous) and accepts either duration or distance as input.

What makes it useful: it calculates calories burned even when you only know duration and intensity — no GPS required.

**Use it for:** indoor trainer sessions where you only have time data, comparing outdoor rides of different intensities, cycling-focused weight management.

[Try it →](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/cycling-burn-calculator)

## 4. Triathlon Calculator

A triathlon has a hidden time cost that most athletes underestimate: **transitions**. In a sprint triathlon, T1 + T2 can add 5–10 minutes — more than the difference between a good and a great overall time.

This tool calculates total race time by summing swim, T1, bike, T2, and run segments. It outputs each segment's time, pace/speed, and the percentage of total time each discipline consumes. For most age-group athletes, bike accounts for ~50% of total time and run about 35%.

What makes it useful: it shows you where to focus training. If your run is 40% of total time but you're spending 60% of your training on the bike, the math suggests a rebalance.

**Use it for:** race pace planning, transition area optimization, training time allocation.

[Try it →](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/triathlon-calculator)

## 5. Marathon Time Predictor

Coaches have used the **Riegel formula** (T2 = T1 × (D2/D1)^1.06) since the 1970s to predict marathon finish times from shorter race performances. It's not perfect, but it's grounded in decades of exercise physiology research.

This tool applies three different prediction formulas — Riegel, Cameron, and Purdue — and averages them. It also outputs split times at every 5 km and per km/mile pacing recommendations.

What makes it useful: different formulas work better for different fitness profiles. The averaged result tends to be more reliable than any single formula.

**Use it for:** setting realistic marathon goals, negative split planning, identifying whether your recent training is on pace for your target.

[Try it →](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/marathon-time)

## 6. Basketball Stats Calculator

Points per game is a terrible way to evaluate a player. A forward who takes 25 shots for 25 points is less efficient than a point guard who takes 12 shots for 18 points — but the box score doesn't show that without calculation.

This tool computes **Player Efficiency Rating (PER)**, True Shooting Percentage (which accounts for free throws and three-pointers), and per-36-minutes production to normalize for players with different minutes. It also gives letter grades for overall rating, scoring, playmaking, and efficiency.

What makes it useful: coaches, fantasy managers, and analysts all use these metrics — now anyone can calculate them in seconds without a spreadsheet.

**Use it for:** player evaluation, fantasy basketball analysis, game tape preparation.

## 7. BMI Calculator for Children

Adult BMI is calculated as weight (kg) / height (m)^2 and interpreted on a single scale. Children are different — a 10-year-old boy with a BMI of 17.5 is in the 50th percentile, while a 17-year-old with the same BMI is in the 5th percentile.

This tool uses **CDC age and gender-specific growth charts** to interpret BMI for ages 2–20. It outputs a percentile rank and a health recommendation based on whether the child falls underweight, healthy weight, overweight, or obese for their age group.

What makes it useful: parents and pediatricians can quickly check whether a child's BMI is on track without needing reference charts or clinical software.

**Use it for:** pediatric health monitoring, school health screenings, parental awareness.

[Try it →](https://elysiatools.com/en/tools/bmi-calculator-kids)

## 8. Football Stats Calculator

Goals per game is the most visible soccer stat, but it tells an incomplete story. A striker with 10 goals from 50 shots is far less valuable than one with 10 goals from 25 shots — and the gap matters for team selection decisions.

This tool calculates goals and assists per 90 minutes, **shooting efficiency** (shots per goal), shot-on-target percentage, and goal conversion rate (shots on target → goals). It also rates players on a World Class → Needs Improvement scale for overall, scoring, efficiency, and playmaking.

What makes it useful: it surfaces the difference between a lucky scorer and an actually productive player — something raw goal counts obscure.

**Use it for:** player scouting, fantasy football analysis, post-match performance review.
