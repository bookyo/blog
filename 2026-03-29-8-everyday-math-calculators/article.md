## 5. Rule of 72 Calculator

Here's a surprising fact: **72 ÷ interest rate = years to double your money.** That's it. No spreadsheet, no compound interest formula. Divide 72 by your annual return and you get a surprisingly accurate estimate.

At 8% annual return: 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years to double. The exact calculation gives 9.01 years. The Rule of 72 was good enough for Einstein, and it's good enough for quick financial planning.

This calculator goes further: it compares **Rule 72, Rule 69, and Rule 70**, shows you the exact calculation, calculates the error percentage for each, and tells you which rule is most accurate for your rate. It also computes triple/quadruple/10x growth timelines and the reverse — what rate you need to double in X years.
