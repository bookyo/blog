## 1. Narrative Economics: SIR Model for Economic Beliefs

**URL:** [https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/narrative-economics](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/narrative-economics)

Robert Shiller's insight was that economic narratives spread like diseases. The Narrative Economics visualization applies the classic **SIR model** (Susceptible → Infected → Recovered) to show how beliefs propagate through society.

**How it works:** You control two key parameters:

- **Contagion rate (c):** How fast the narrative spreads. Social media amplify this dramatically—influencer endorsements, viral shares, and algorithmic amplification all increase *c*.
- **Recovery rate (r):** How fast people stop believing. Counter-evidence, competing narratives, and simple attention decay drive this.

The **basic reproduction number R₀ = c/r** determines everything. When R₀ > 1, you get an epidemic of belief. When R₀ < 1, the narrative dies out.

**Real cases included:** The tool documents historical "narrative epidemics"—the Laffer Curve in the 1970s-80s, the Great Recession comparison to 1929 in 2008-09, and Bitcoin's viral "digital gold" narrative in 2017. Each case shows peak infection timing and duration.

**Use this when:** You want to understand *why* certain economic ideas suddenly dominate public discourse—or why they fade. The model explains why the same policy proposal can seem revolutionary one decade and obvious the next.

## 2. Fifth Consumption Era: From Material Accumulation to Well-Being

**URL:** [https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/fifth-consumption-era](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/fifth-consumption-era)

Japanese sociologist Atsushi Miura identified five consumption eras in modern history. The Fifth (2021–2043) represents a fundamental shift: from material accumulation to spiritual fulfillment and well-being.

**The 7S Framework:** The visualization lets you explore seven interconnected values:

**Why it matters:** This framework explains phenomena like the "lying flat" movement, the rise of wellness tourism, and why Gen Z is driving demand for sustainable and experience-based consumption over material goods.

**Use this when:** You're building products or services for the next decade. The framework predicts which consumer values will dominate as aging societies shift priorities from "having" to "being."

## 3. Two-Sided Markets: Network Effects and Platform Economics

**URL:** [https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/two-sided-markets](https://elysiatools.com/en/visualizations/two-sided-markets)

Why does Uber charge riders more when there are fewer drivers? Why does Amazon sometimes sell below cost? Why do app stores take 30% of every sale? The answer lies in **two-sided market dynamics**—and this visualization makes them tangible.

**Core concepts:** Platform businesses connect two groups (buyers and sellers, riders and drivers, advertisers and users). The key strategic question: **which side do you subsidize to trigger the feedback loop?**

- **Cross-side network effects:** More riders attract more drivers, which attracts more riders.
- **Same-side network effects:** More buyers help other buyers (through reviews, liquidity), but more sellers can hurt each other (increased competition).

**Interactive simulations:** You can manipulate pricing strategies, subsidy structures, and growth flywheel parameters to see how platform dominance emerges—or collapses.

**Use this when:** You're analyzing any platform business, evaluating a startup's unit economics, or trying to understand why tech giants behave the way they do. The visualization makes abstract game theory concrete.
